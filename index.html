What is Naegleria fowleri?

Naegleria fowleri, often referred to as the "brain-eating amoeba," is a microscopic single-celled organism that thrives in warm freshwater environments.

Where it is Found

It lives in warm lakes, rivers, canals, ponds, and hot springs, particularly in locations with temperatures above 25°C. The amoeba is also found in soil and mud.

The Disease it Causes

When contaminated water enters the nose, the amoeba can travel to the brain, causing a rare and almost always fatal brain infection known as **Primary Amebic Meningoencephalitis (PAM)**.

High Fatality Rate

While infections are very rare, PAM has a fatality rate of over 97%. This is why awareness and prevention are absolutely critical, especially during the summer months.
